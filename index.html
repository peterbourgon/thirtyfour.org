When we step into the world and become an anonymous extra, sonder to the lives of all those we see, we are in a fugue state, known only through the reflected gaze. In this isolation there is a safety, a sociocultural barrier. Sitting or walking or being alone, I am an autonomous and ambulatory actor, with purpose but without meaning. Here I can contemplate, like Atlas, and be contemplated in turn. Here I perceive my alienation most acutely: the infinite and fractal complexity of my own thoughts, projected outward as a blank stare, a bite of a sandwich, the occupation of space and nothing beyond.
